Materials on the outside of the building

The outside walls are covered by:

  • 60% glass
  • 35% concrete
  • 5% aluminium composite material (ACM)

The aluminium composite material:

  • has met the required fire classification A2-s1, d0 or better - this classifies building materials based on their reaction to fire
  • has passed a large scale fire test to BS8414, the British Standard for tests that assess the fire safety of cladding applied to the outside of buildings

  • 50% foil bubble or multifoil - this is metal foil with air pockets or other insulating material inside
  • 50% fibreglass or mineral wool fibre

Features on the building

There are balconies made mostly from concrete and glass.

There are sky lights made mostly from glass and plastic.

Changes made to the building

We do not hold information about day-to-day maintenance and repairs. We only hold information about large scale works that needed special permission (building control approval) and affected large areas of the building.

These changes have been made to the building since it was built:

  • asbestos was removed
  • more floors were added - these floors were built from a metal other than steel which was cast into modular or prefabricated blocks and fastened together
  • work was done to the cold water systems in more than one of the flats or other residential units

The most recent of these changes was when asbestos was removed in 1999.

If you think any of this information is wrong

If any of this information has changed, it can take up to 10 weeks to show here.

If you are part of Greenways Limited

As the principal accountable person, you must tell the Building Safety Regulator if any of this information has changed. Find out more about making changes to your registration.

If you are a resident

If you live in Avon Court and think any of this information has changed, contact Greenways Limited. Tell them what you think has changed and ask them to change it. They should have shared their engagement strategy with all residents of this building. The engagement strategy should tell you how to complain or ask them questions.

I have tried and failed to contact Greenways Limited
If you have contacted Greenways Limited, but not had a response, contact the Building Safety Regulator. If they have not already told us about the change, we will contact them about it.
