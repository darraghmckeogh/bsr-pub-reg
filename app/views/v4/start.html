Information the Building Safety Regulator holds about high-rise residential buildings

Find out what information the Building Safety Regulator holds in its register of high-rise residential buildings in England, and how you can access that information.

From: Health and Safety Executive
Published 20 September 2023

Applies to England

Buildings in this register

The Building Safety Regulator holds information about high-rise residential buildings in England. The buildings must have been registered with us and must have:

  • at least 7 floors (or be at least 18 metres in height)
  • at least 2 residential units
  • people living in them or about to move in

Information the Building Safety Regulator holds

We hold information about who is responsible for the safety of the building's structure, external walls and roof. We call this organisation or person the ‘principal accountable person’.

They are also responsible for registering the building with the Building Safety Regulator. And they must tell us if any of the information we hold about the building changes.

We also hold information about other organisations or people who share responsibility for the safety of the parts of the building shared by residents. We call these organisations and people the ‘accountable persons’.

If the principal accountable person does not share responsibility for the building’s safety with anyone else, the building might not have any other accountable persons.

For each structure within a building, we also hold information about the building’s height, the number of floors and when it was built.

Search for a high-rise residential building

You can find the information we hold about a high-rise residential building if you know its postcode.

If you think the information about the building is wrong

You must try to contact the principal accountable person. They are responsible for the safety of the building's structure, external walls and roof. They are also responsible for registering the building and telling us if any of the information changes.

If you need help using this service

If you need help or need information about more than one building at a time, contact the Building Safety Regulator.
