Find the information that the Building Safety Regulator holds about a high-rise residential building in England.

A high-rise residential building has:

  • at least 7 floors or is at least 18 metres in height
  • at least 2 residential units
  • people living in it or about to move in

You can find out who is responsible for the safety of the building's structure and the parts shared by residents. This organisation or person is also responsible for registering the building with the Buiding Safety Regulator.

You can also find information about:

  • the building - like, its height, the number of floors and when it was built
  • the fire evacuation strategy and equipment to control fire and smoke
  • materials used in the building
  • what the building is used for
  • if and how it's connnected to other buildings
  • the building's structure
  • energy supplies and storage

You'll need to know the name, address or postcode of the building.
