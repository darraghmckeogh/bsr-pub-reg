Evacuation or firefighter lifts

The building contains at least one:

  • fire fighting lift
  • firemen's lift
What are the different types of evacuation or firefighters lift?

Evacuation lifts have structural, electrical and fire protection. They're used to evacuate people with a disability or extra needs.

Firefighters lifts have protection, controls and signals for firefighters to control it. The 2003 British Standard classifies these lifts.

Fire-fighting lifts are like firefighters lifts but less stringent. The 1986 British Standard classifies these lifts.

Modernised lifts for fire service use are normally used for passengers. But they have basic extra protection, controls and signals for firefighters to use.

Firemen's lifts were installed before fire-fighting lift standards were available. They have no complex lift controls or protection measures for firefighters to use.

Previous work done to the building

Asbestos removal and remediation was completed in 1999.

More floors were previously added. They were structured from a concrete large panel system from 1970 or later.

Work was previously done to heating and hot water systems in multiple residential units.

Use of the building

The floors above ground are mainly used for residential dwellings, like flats. It is also used for:

  • offices
  • shop and commercial, like retail businesses, restaurants or hairdressers

There is 1 floor below ground that is used for other non-residential purposes.

It used to be used as a residential institution until 1998.

Connections to other buildings

Avon Court is part of Aston Heights.

The structures of Aston Heights are connected by:

  • a bridge or walkway
  • a car park below ground
  • a shared wall or shared walls with at least one emergency door

Aston Heights is connected to other high-rise residential buildings at ground floor.

Aston Heights is not connected to any other buildings.
